Did Travis Kelce have a reality show?

Travis Kelce, the Kansas City Chiefs tight end, indeed had a reality show called "Catching Kelce." 

The Reality Show: "Catching Kelce"

In 2016, Travis Kelce was the star of the E! reality dating series "Catching Kelce," where 50 women competed for his affections.

The show's gimmick was that one woman from each state would vie for the title of Kelce's "first girlfriend in the NFL" without the promise of an engagement or a cash prize.

Kelce's Initial Reluctance and Show's Reception

Kelce initially turned down the show numerous times due to financial stability and concerns about the nature of the show.

Although, he eventually agreed after learning about the potential six-figure earnings and the involvement of 50 women.

The show, "Catching Kelce," received mixed reviews, with some calling it "pretty rough viewing".

Kelce himself admitted that the show was worse than the reality series "Love Is Blind" but also expressed that watching "trash" TV like this could be enjoyable.

Impact on Kelce's Career and Personal Life

While the show may not have been critically acclaimed, it undeniably brought Kelce into the limelight and helped him gain recognition beyond the football field. 

The show not only impacted Kelce's public image but also his personal life. It's worth noting that Kelce's relationship with one of the contestants, Maya Benberry, became a topic of public interest.

Kelce's Outlook on Reality TV

Despite the experience of "Catching Kelce," Kelce has expressed his reluctance to return to reality TV, stating that his team has no desire for another reality television run.

He has also acknowledged that the show made him more comfortable with the idea of taking similar opportunities in the future.

Future Endeavors in Entertainment

In 2024, reports emerged that Kelce was in talks to host a celebrity-focused reboot of "Are You Smarter Than a Fifth Grader?" on Amazon Prime Video.

This potential move further solidifies Kelce's foray into the entertainment industry beyond his football career.

Travis Kelce indeed had a reality show called "Catching Kelce." Despite its mixed reception, the show played a significant role in shaping Kelce's public image and opened doors for potential opportunities in the entertainment industry.

While Kelce has expressed his reluctance to return to reality TV, his involvement in discussions to host a game show reboot indicates his willingness to explore new avenues in the entertainment world.

Overall, "Catching Kelce" may have been a brief chapter in Kelce's career, but it undeniably contributed to his journey beyond the realm of professional football.
