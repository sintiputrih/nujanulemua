Do ants eat plants?

September 2024 · 6 minute read
Aside from their tendency to bite us, ants can cause other problems in the garden. Ants are fond of the sweet sap of host plants. A diligent group of ants chewing on a plant's stem or trunk is capable of girdling and even killing a garden plant. In addition, ants will cultivate and protect other damaging garden pests.

    Will ants eat my tomato plants?

    When ants invade your tomato plants, don't be alarmed -- it's unlikely that these pests are there to take over. Instead, the ants are indicating the presence of aphids or whiteflies, sap-feeding pests that produce copious amounts of honeydew, a substance that ants use for food.

    Does killing an ant attract more?

    Yes, killing ants is likely to attract more ants from the nearby colony. Ants communicate through smells. They emit different chemicals that produce different scents to send different signals. The alarm pheromone is the strongest and travels fastest and farthest, prompting other ants to act immediately.
