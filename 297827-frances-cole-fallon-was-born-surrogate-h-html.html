Frances Cole Fallon Was Born via Surrogate after Her Parents Struggled with Infertility - Meet Jimmy

February 2024 · 4 minute read

Winnie Rose and Frances Cole Fallon Smile and Pose for a Picture | Jimmy Fallon on “The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon” | Source: Instagram/jimmyfallon | Getty Images

Frances Cole Fallon is the youngest of the Fallon family, and alongside her older sister, Winnie, she has developed a personality that resembles her dad's. She showcased her musical talents, appearing on the "At Home Edition" of her dad's show during the height of the COVID-19 pandemic.

Frances Cole Fallon drew her first breath on December 3, 2014, born to Jimmy Fallon and Nancy Juvonen. The now-7-year-old weighed 5 pounds and 11 ounces and measured 18.5 inches in height. However, her parents kept her conception and arrival a secret until after she was born.

The day after Frances Cole entered the world, her dad shared a sweet tweet on her behalf, saying she "sends her thanks." The father of two also revealed his nickname for his secondborn, as he affectionately referred to her as "Franny."

Frances Cole Fallon's Parents Did Not Give Up until She Was Born via Surrogate

Three days after Frances Cole was born, her dad displayed her newborn pictures upon his return on his show. As Jimmy gushed about Frances Cole, calling her a "cute little baby," he expressed his gratitude for the "angels in Texas," who contributed to the successful arrival of his daughter.

The arrival of Frances Cole was a big deal for her parents, as they had struggled with fertility issues in their efforts to have kids. They opened up about those issues during an "At Home Edition" episode of Jimmy's "The Tonight Show" in April 2020.

As they strolled their neighborhood streets, Jimmy and Nancy revealed that having children had always been a shared goal for them as a couple. As Nancy explained, it wasn't even a matter of asking each other if they were ready but of how they would have children.

Jimmy Fallon and his daughter, Frances, are pictured greeting the audience during the Grand Opening of Universal Orlando's Newest Attraction "Race Through New York Starring Jimmy Fallon" at Universal Orlando on April 6, 2017, in Orlando, Florida | Source: Getty Images

For years, however, the couple struggled and were reluctant to go for alternative birth options. Frances Cole's parents also emphasized the emotional turmoil they underwent while trying to conceive.

At one point, her mom tried different "weird teas" that would make her cry "fourteen times a day." On the other hand, her dad would sleep upside down while dangling at his feet from the ceiling. He also put magnets in his underwear.

Still, the emotional weight of failed efforts was heavy for Jimmy and Nancy; they needed to cope. Every time received bad news regarding their efforts to conceive, they would go away to destress for a weekend before returning home to try again.

Eventually, they decided to go for surrogacy and had both Frances Cole and their eldest, Winnie Rose, through that alternative. Despite their fertility hurdles, Frances Cole's parents still encouraged their audience to persevere until their dream of parenthood was a reality.

Frances Cole Fallon Is Already Following in Her Dad's Footsteps at a Young Age

Frances Cole made regular appearances on her dad's "At Home Edition" in 2020 as both a guest host and – alongside Winnie Rose – she became a member of the show's band. She often stole the spotlight with her goofy giggles and interruptions while her dad interviewed celebrities like Ciara and Russell Wilson.

Jennifer Garner also appeared in "At Home Edition," having a mini jam session with Jimmy, as she played the saxophone while he played the guitar. One of the host's daughters interrupted the session, asking her dad how she could "change her character."

Frances Cole Fallon Often Enjoys Fun Family Times with Her Parents & Sister

It seems there's never a dull moment with the Fallon family, as Frances Cole's dad's Instagram suggests. In January this year, Jimmy posted an adorable picture of Frances Cole and her older sister playing on their iPads while sitting back-to-back on his back and wearing matching night dresses.

That same month, he shared a photo – that Nancy Juvonen was too embarrassed to be a part of – of himself, Frances Cole, and Winnie Rose wearing matching snowsuits. In April, Jimmy showed his audience photos he took with his family around Washington, DC, before they attended President Joe Biden's Easter Egg Roll.
