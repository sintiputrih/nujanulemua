After graduating from college, Tosh worked as a telemarketer until he left for Los Angeles to achieve his dream of becoming a stand-up comedian on the comedy club tour.

Career breakthrough

Tosh’s major breakthrough in comedy occurred in 2001 when he was featured on the Late Show with David Letterman.

Following his appearance on the Late Show, Tosh performed on hit talk shows like Jimmy Kimmel Live!.

Simultaneously, he was presenting Tens, a late-night comedy program in Florida, and voicing Taco Bell advertisements.

Evidently, Tosh has appeared on several radio programs, including The Bob & Tom Show and Loveline.

Consequently, the star comedian Tosh received the chance to appear in his own 30-minute program on Comedy Central Originals.

In addition, his first stand-up special was entitled Daniel Tosh: Completely Serious, premiered on Comedy Central in June 2007.

Ascend to fame and fortune

Daniel Tosh’s popularity continued to rise over time. In addition, Tosh launched his first comedy album, “True Stories I Made Up,” in 2005, which was a huge hit.

Following that, he was voted one of the “Top 10 Stand-up Comedian to Watch” in the same year.

Later, the stand-up comic released his second comedy album “Completely Serious” in 2007, and his third “Happy Thoughts” in 2011.

Nevertheless, he has also appeared in several stand-up comedy shows, notably Comedy Central Presents: Daniel Tosh: Happy Thoughts in 2011 and Daniel Tosh: People Pleaser in 2016.

Not to mention his part as Malloy in the television comedy Brickle Berry, which he executive produced from 2012 to 2015.

In fact, He was nominated for Teen Choice Awards three years in a row (2011, 2012, and 2013).

Evidently, he developed himself as a renowned comedian, and the average ticket price for his concert in 2014 was more than $160.

In addition, in 2008, he got a short role in the movie called The Love Guru, which was his sole film appearance as of this writing.

On June 5, 2009, Comedy Central network broadcasted Daniel Tosh’s first-ever TV show called Tosh.0.

Subsequently, Tosh.0 turned into one of the top series on the Comedy Central network, which is no surprise.

In addition, Tosh is also a frequent guest star on The Bob & Tom Show.
