Vinessa Shaw is an American actress and model who is best known for her work in films such as Ladybugs (1992), Hocus Pocus (1993), L.A. Without a Map (1998), Eyes Wide Shut (1999), 40 Days and 40 Nights (2002), The Hills Have Eyes (2006), 3:10 to Yuma (2007), Two Lovers (2008), and Clinical (2017). She is also well recognized for her portrayal of the character of Kate McPherson in the crime drama TV series Ray Donovan (2014).

    Vinessa Shaw Facts

  • Her family’s original surname was ‘Schwartz’ which later got deformed into ‘Shaw’.
  • Her first name, Vinessa, is spelled with an ‘i’ rather than an ‘a’ as it is a variation of Vincent, her maternal grandfather’s first name.
  • Her first formal performance came in a short in a UCLA (University of California, Los Angeles) acting camp when she was 10 years old. About a year later, she toured with children’s folk singer Peter Alsop.
  • In 1989, aged 13, she signed up with Elite Model Management and did a few modeling assignments and commercial work, before the start of her acting career.
  • Vinessa was raised a Buddhist and she embraced the Nichiren Buddhist philosophy as a member of the Soka Gakkai International (an international Nichiren Buddhist organization that was founded in 1975) when she was in her first year of college (1996).
  • As it is a core practice of Nichiren Buddhism, she chants Daimoku daily. ‘Namu Myōhō Renge Kyō’ (Devotion to the Mystic Law of the Lotus Sutra) is a Japanese phrase that is chanted within all forms of Nichiren Buddhism. ‘Myōhō Renge Kyō’ refers to the Japanese title of the Lotus Sūtra. The mantra is referred to as Daimoku.
